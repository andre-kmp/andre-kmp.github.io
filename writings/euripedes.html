14 lessons from Eurípedes

July 2026.

Between 2021 and 2022, I was Automation Engineering intern, and our team included a consultant named Eurípedes Nóbrega. At the time, he was a post-doc in engineering (I wouldn't be surprised if he had earned another degree since then) and had solid experience across various engineering fields.

The team held a weekly meeting, and I used to write down phrases spoken by Eurípedes. Those phrases resembled aphorisms - concise and deeply meaningfully - and, as such, the wisdom they contained could be applied to areas besides beyond those for which they were originally intended.

I am glad to present those statements here. For each one, I add the context in which it was spoken - doing so from memory, 5 years later - and an extrapolation of its aphoristic meaning.

  1. One is the same as none.

    From the original in portuguese: "Um é igual a nenhum".

    He spoke this phrase more than once, usually highlighting the need to collect data from more than one device; after all, if only one device is collecting data, how can you tell if a hardware fault is corrupting data?

    This also makes me reflect is about the importance of having more than one advisor for life's challenges. We never know whether the advice stems from an objective analysis of the situation or from the person's own subjective reaction. Talking to more than one person helps distinguish between them two.

  2. To drive the final nail into the coffin, there can be no doubt.

    From the original in portuguese: "Para bater o prego no caixão não pode haver nenhuma dúvida".

    The context of this teaching was the final stage of the prototype development. The engineerings were discussing the product's housing, but some tests remained inconclusive. Eurípedes seemed concerned about the possibility of designing and mounting the product inside a housing, to have to redo everything later.

    To me, it is obvious that some decisions are hard to reverse, and we need to be fully aware of this, since suchbacktrackings consume a lot of time and energy.

  3. The lighter, the better.

    From the original in portuguese: "Quanto mais leve, melhor".

    The context here was design of the product as a whole. There were many trade-offs to consider, and Eurípedes provided this razor. Reducing mass is a powerful form of optimization in engineering.

    This applies well to solutions in general. Sometimes, weight isn't a matter of physical mass - it can be excess of information, friction, volume, etc.

  4. It is much easier to improve something that already has a beginning, a middle, and an end.

    From the original in portuguese: "É muito mais fácil melhorar uma coisa que já tem começo, meio e fim".

    Here, he was explaining the need to finalize a first version of our prototype, so we could start improving it.

    There is no much extrapolation to add here.

  5. If it is working, leave it working.

    From the original in portuguese: "Se está funcionando, deixe funcionando".

    This happened during a discussion about restarting the work in a component of our product. He was against it - we had a lot to do, and redoing what was working would mean losing time.

    I'm not 100% in because I'm a huge fan of refactoring. Maybe a golden rule would be: if everything is finished, then you can refactor.

  6. It is much easier to refine something than to kickstart it.

    From the original in portuguese: "É muito mais fácil aperfeiçoar do que dar um chute inicial".

    This statement is a complement of number 4.

  7. The most useful monitoring of a work environment is based in the prediction of anomalies.

    From the original in portuguese: "O monitoramento mais útil de um ambiente de trabalho se baseia na previsão daquilo que é anormal".

  8. You are only capable of discovering where to go if you are already somewhere.

    From the original in portuguese: "Você só consegue descobrir a próxima pedra para pisar se já estiver em cima de uma".

  9. Academic researchers present the tendency of worrying about they don’t know, while the market has a tendency of worrying about they know.

    From the original in portuguese: "Pesquisadores têm uma tendência a se preocupar com o que não sabem, enquanto o mercado tem uma tendência a se preocupar com o que sabe".

  10. Profit comes from what we know.

    From the original in portuguese: "O lucro vem daquilo que sabemos".

  11. Every system needs to monitor itself.

    From the original in portuguese: "Todo sistema precisa monitorar a si próprio".

  12. Monitoring must be based on two concepts: symptoms and diagnosis.

    From the original in portuguese: "Monitoramento deve ser baseado em dois conceitos: sintoma e diagnóstico".

  13. It is important to have redundancy because redundancy creates security.

    From the original in portuguese: "É importante ter redundância porque a redundância gera segurança".

  14. The worst defect a product can exhibit is instability.

    From the original in portuguese: "O pior defeito que um produto pode apresentar é a instabilidade".
